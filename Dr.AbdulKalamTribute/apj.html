DR. APJ ABDUL KALAM

The Missile Man of India.

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - Graduates from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics.
  • 1955 - Moves to Madras (now Chennai) to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - Joins the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Service (DRDS).
  • 1969 - Transfers to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he becomes project director of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1974 - Leads the team that successfully launches India's first satellite, Rohini-1, into space.
  • 1980 - Appointed as the Chief Executive of Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • 1983 - Overseeing the successful launch of the first satellite launch vehicle SLV-III.
  • 1992 - Becomes the Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1997 - Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007.
  • 2007 - Returns to a life of education, writing, and public service after his presidency.
  • 2015 - Passes away in Shillong, Meghalaya, India, leaving behind a legacy of scientific achievement, education advocacy, and inspirational leadership.
